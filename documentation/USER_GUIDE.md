# Accessibility Lens — User Guide

This guide explains how to use Accessibility Lens with gestures, voice commands, and settings.

## What the app does

Accessibility Lens uses your phone camera to:
- Describe what is in front of you (scene description).
- Read text in view (OCR + cleaned text-to-speech).
- Provide safety guidance when the camera is covered or the environment is too dark.


### How to open the application
- Open the application using **Google Voice Assistant**

## Main gestures (Home screen)

### Single tap — Describe scene
- Captures a photo and speaks a short scene description.
- Intended to include hazard awareness (for example, tripping hazards) when possible.

What you’ll hear:
- “Describing scene” followed by the description.

### Double tap — Read text
- Captures a photo and attempts to detect and read text out loud.

What you’ll hear:
- “Capturing text”
- If text is found: “The text says: …”
- If no text is found: “No text detected in view.”

### Triple tap — Repeat last message
- Repeats the most recently spoken message.

### Long press (hold) — Voice commands (“hold to talk”)
- Press and hold to speak a command.
- Release to stop listening.

Examples:
- “Capture scene”
- “Capture text”
- “Volume up”
- “Stop”
- “Help”

### Swipe up / Swipe down — Change speech rate
- Swipe up: faster speech
- Swipe down: slower speech

### Swipe left / Swipe right — Speech off/on
- Swipe left: disable speech output (TTS off)
- Swipe right: enable speech output (TTS on)

Important:
- When speech is off, the app may still give haptic taps and will remember the last message for repeat.

## Voice command list

Say one of the following while holding to talk:

- Capture scene / Describe scene / “What’s around me”
- Capture text / Read text / “What does this say”
- Volume up / “Louder”
- Volume down / “Quieter”
- Stop (stops current speech)
- Repeat (repeats last message)
- Help (reads available commands)
- Exit / Close app / Quit (exits the app)
- Force stop / Reset app / Restart app (reinitializes camera monitoring)

Notes:
- Commands have built-in cooldowns to prevent accidental rapid repeats.
- If you say something unknown, the app will ignore it (to avoid noisy feedback).

## Status messages you may hear/see

- “Ready” — idle and ready for interaction
- “Analyzing scene…” — capturing and describing
- “Reading text…” — capturing and reading
- “Listening…” — voice command listening mode

Camera guidance messages (if enabled):
- “Lens is covered. Please uncover the camera.”
- “It is too dark. Please turn on a light or move to a brighter area.”
- “Good lighting detected”

## Settings overview

Open Settings using the gear icon.

### Speech
- Text to speech: turn spoken feedback on or off
- Voice: choose a voice by locale and name
- Speech rate: adjusts how fast the voice speaks
- Pitch: adjusts voice pitch

### Feedback
- Haptics: vibration feedback on/off
- Camera guidance: lens blocked / too dark alerts

## Tips for best results

- Hold the phone steady during capture.
- Make sure the camera lens is not covered.
- Use good lighting when reading text.
- For text: aim so the text fills the camera view and is not too far away.
